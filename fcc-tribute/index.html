Bugs

The Icon. The Bunny.

Bugs in classic action.

Bugs has been a tremendously influential character to the young and old alike. First appearing in 1939, Bugs brought laughter to several generations over a span of 78 years. He starred in countless cartoons, commercials, and silver screen movies. You can find this rabbit on mugs, lunchboxes, t-shirts, sweaters, and he may even be lurking on the boxers of that guy at work. He is an iconic character who shall never be forgotten.

Here is a brief timeline of Bugs' development over the decades...

  • 1939: Bugs is born! He is created by Ben "Bugs" Hardaway as a replacement for Daffy Duck in "Porky's Hare Hunt."
  • 1940: Bugs's personality is developed, at the hands of Tex Avery, with his first line being "What's up, Doc?", voiced by Mel Blanc for the first time, in "A Wild Hare."
  • 1943: Bugs receives his iconic look.
  • 1945: Bugs provides entertainment to the soldiers in WWII and becomes a national mascot.
  • 1964: The "Golden Age" of cartoons goes on decline. Bugs is not seen again in new content until 1976.
  • 1985: Bugs gets a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 1996: Space Jam...
  • 1997: Bugs is the first cartoon character to be honored by being placed on a US postage stamp.
  • 2004: "Hare and Loathing in Las Vegas" becomes the last animated cartoon in which Bugs starred.
